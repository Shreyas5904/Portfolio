Linear Induction Motor Assembly Design

2023 - 2024 Duke Hyperloop Team

Overview

Custom Linear Induction Motor (LIM) assembly design for Duke University's Hyperloop competition team. This project involved designing a robust and modular motor system capable of withstanding significant electromagnetic forces while maintaining precise alignment with the track structure.

The primary objective was to create a propulsion system that could accelerate a Hyperloop pod to competition speeds while maintaining structural integrity, thermal management, and ease of assembly/disassembly for rapid prototyping and testing cycles.

Project Definition & Requirements

The motor assembly required meeting several critical engineering specifications:

  • Withstand electromagnetic forces without deformation or failure
  • Maintain precise alignment with track structure (±2mm tolerance)
  • Integrate seamlessly with electrical and control systems
  • Allow for quick assembly and disassembly during testing
  • Provide thermal management for sustained operation
  • Minimize weight while maintaining structural rigidity

Design Process

CAD Modeling & Design

  • Comprehensive CAD modeling using SolidWorks
  • Motor coil housing design optimized for electromagnetic performance
  • Mechanical mounting structure for track integration
  • Thermal pathway design for heat dissipation
  • Modular assembly design for manufacturing flexibility

Tolerance Analysis & GD&T

  • Applied Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing (GD&T) principles
  • Defined critical tolerances for motor alignment and positioning
  • Stack-up analysis to ensure assembly compatibility
  • Manufacturing compliance verification

Structural Analysis

  • Finite Element Analysis (FEA) for stress and deformation prediction
  • Electromagnetic force analysis and load cases
  • Material selection for optimal strength-to-weight ratio
  • Vibration analysis to minimize resonance issues

Pod Rig assembly showing track and mechanical mounting

Manufacturing & Fabrication

The motor assembly was fabricated using precision machining and additive manufacturing techniques:

  • CNC machining for high-precision metal components
  • Fastener placement and specification optimization
  • Assembly jig design for production efficiency
  • Quality control inspection protocols
  • Integration testing with electrical subsystems

System Integration & Testing

The motor assembly was integrated with the broader Hyperloop pod systems:

  • Electrical sub-team coordination for power delivery and control
  • Track integration and alignment verification
  • Thermal testing to validate heat dissipation
  • Performance testing under operational loads
  • Modular design validation for rapid reconfiguration

Results & Key Achievements

  • Designed robust motor assembly capable of withstanding electromagnetic forces
  • Achieved ±2mm alignment tolerance with track structure
  • Successfully integrated with propulsion and electrical systems
  • Enabled rapid assembly/disassembly for iterative testing
  • Contributed to competition performance and system reliability
