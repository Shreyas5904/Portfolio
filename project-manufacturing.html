Advanced Riblet Surface Manufacturing

Aug 2024 - Present Duke Aeroelasticity Lab

Overview

High-precision manufacturing of riblet surfaces using advanced additive manufacturing techniques. This project focuses on achieving consistent surface quality and dimensional accuracy for aerodynamic testing applications, with emphasis on reproducibility and quality control in production processes.

The goal is to develop manufacturing protocols that can reliably produce riblet-structured surfaces suitable for wind tunnel testing and real-world aerodynamic applications, while maintaining tight tolerances and surface finish requirements.

Manufacturing Objectives

The manufacturing project aimed to address key challenges in riblet surface production:

  • Achieve high-resolution 3D printing with consistent surface quality
  • Develop protocols for both rigid and flexible riblet materials
  • Implement robust quality control measures
  • Optimize printing parameters for fine-scale surface features
  • Create reproducible manufacturing processes for validation testing

Manufacturing Process

Design to Manufacturing

  • CAD model preparation and optimization for 3D printing
  • Slicing and support structure generation
  • Print parameter optimization (layer height, exposure time, etc.)
  • Material selection based on application requirements

Additive Manufacturing

  • High-resolution 3D printing technology deployment
  • Multi-material printing capability for different applications
  • In-situ quality monitoring during production
  • Real-time parameter adjustment for optimization

Post-Processing & Finishing

  • Support structure removal and cleaning procedures
  • Surface finishing and polishing techniques
  • Dimensional verification and inspection
  • Final quality assurance protocols

Quality Control & Metrology

Comprehensive quality control measures ensure consistency and reliability:

  • Dimensional accuracy verification using precision instruments
  • Surface roughness measurement and analysis
  • Visual inspection for defects and imperfections
  • Statistical process control for batch production
  • Traceability and documentation for all manufactured samples

Material Studies & Optimization

Different materials were evaluated for specific applications:

  • Rigid riblet materials for structural durability
  • Flexible riblet materials for adaptive surfaces
  • Material properties testing and validation
  • Cost-benefit analysis for production scaling
  • Environmental considerations and sustainability

Results & Key Achievements

  • Achieved high-resolution 3D printing with consistent surface quality across test samples
  • Developed manufacturing protocols for both rigid and flexible riblet materials
  • Implemented comprehensive quality control measures for dimensional accuracy and surface finish
  • Optimized printing parameters for fine-scale surface features and geometric precision
  • Created reproducible manufacturing process validated through multiple production runs
  • Enabled seamless transition from research to production-scale manufacturing
