Next-Generation Solar Cell Materials Research

Jan 2023 - Jul 2023 Volker Blum Lab, Duke University

Overview

Advanced materials science research focusing on chalcogenides and oxide perovskites as potential silicon alternatives in photovoltaic applications. This project contributed to the development of next-generation solar cell technologies with improved efficiency and manufacturing scalability.

The research aimed to identify materials with optimal band gaps for solar energy conversion, analyze industrial scalability, and contribute to a globally accessible research database for the scientific community.

Research Objectives

The research project focused on several key objectives in materials science:

  • Identify potential chalcogenide and oxide perovskite materials for solar cells
  • Analyze band gap characteristics for photovoltaic applications
  • Evaluate industrial scalability and manufacturing feasibility
  • Assess cost-effectiveness of alternative materials
  • Contribute to globally accessible research databases

Advanced perovskite and chalcogenide materials for next-generation solar cells

Materials Analysis & Characterization

Chalcogenides

  • Manufacturing process compatibility evaluation
  • Cost analysis for large-scale production

Oxide Perovskites

Computational Analysis

  • First-principles calculations for material properties

Database Development & Management

A critical component of this research involved developing and maintaining a comprehensive materials database:

  • Muchas materials database management and maintenance

  • DataGrip for database administration and querying
  • GitHub version control for database updates and collaboration
  • Data validation and quality assurance protocols
